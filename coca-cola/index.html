Our Company

Coca-Cola was created in 1886 by Dr. John Stith Pemberton in Atlanta, Georgia, USA. Coca-Cola was first bottled in 1864 in Vickburg, Mississippi. Coca-Cola owns more than 500 brands, including Sprite, Fanta, Minute Maid, Powerade, and Dasani. The Coca-Cola logo was designed in the Spencerian script style, which was popular for business correspondence in the late 19th century.

During World War II, Coca-Cola committed to ensuring every U.S. soldier could buy a Coke for 5 cents, which helped the brand expand globally. Coca-Cola became the first soda consumed in space aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger in 1985.

Our Story

Coca-Cola has been a symbol of refreshment for over a century. From humble beginnings to becoming the most recognized brand in the world, our story is one of innovation, community, and timeless taste. Pemberton originally sold Coca-Cola in his pharmacy for five cents per glass. It was marketed as a "brain tonic" and "nerve stimulant".
